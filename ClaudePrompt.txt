## 2. THE THREE UNDERLINE CLASSES — EXACT RULES
Only 3 span classes exist. Using anything else = instant failure.

### `u-blue-zig` — wavy blue — CORE CONTENT (this is the default)
- All meaningful content: nouns, noun phrases, facts, outcomes, concepts, descriptions.
- Spans = 2-5 words each. Break longer content into multiple spans.
- Articles ("The", "A", "An") MAY start a content span: `<span class="u-blue-zig">The British </span>`
- Preposition + object MAY stay together when they form a tight locative unit: `<span class="u-blue-zig">in India </span>`
- Adjectives stay with their content noun inside the same blue-zig span.
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

### `u-green` — solid green — FLOW / STRUCTURAL PHRASES
- Words that connect, transition, or frame — but carry NO factual content.
- Green spans should PRIMARILY be 2-6 word natural grammatical phrases, and also can be isolated single words as per given rules.
- Categories:
  - Linking/structural verbs: unfolded, encompassed, sparked, characterized, marked, and, between, or, against, versus, over, with
  - Participial connectors: ", marked by ", ", sparking the", ", leading to "
  - Infinitive phrases: "to control ", "to protect ", "to establish "
  - Relative clause openers: "who joined the", "which led to", "that resulted in"
  - Discourse markers: "according to", "for instance", "as a result", "however", "therefore"
  - Auxiliary constructions: "was known as", "is called", "are described as", "as", "like"

**CORRECT green phrases (study these — use your own reasoning to match this quality):**
  - "This set the stage for" (4 words)
  - "was characterised by" (3 words)
  - "seeking to establish" (3 words)
  - "often called" (2 words)
  - "which led to the" (4 words)
  - "resulted in the" (3 words)
  - "can be attributed to" (4 words)
  - "played a crucial role in" (5 words)
  - "is considered to be" (4 words)
  - "as a result of" (4 words)
  - "was known for" (3 words)
  - "contributed to the" (3 words)
  - "it includes" (2 words)

**Single-word green ONLY when** a word genuinely stands alone as a structural/flow element between two content spans with no natural way to merge it into a multi-word phrase:
- Structural verbs: "founded," "introduced," "encompassed," "established," "including"
- Bridge conjunctions: "and", "or"
- Bridge prepositions: "against", "between", "versus", "over", "with"
- Standalone structural prepositions: "to", "for", "about", "at", "under", "from", "by", "on", "as", "in"
- Quantifiers/framers: "Both", "Only", "Also"
- Comparators: "unlike", "than"
- Relative/clause words: "where", "that", "which"

- Punctuation (comma, period) bare text outside any span.
- Dashes (—) between list items = bare text outside any span.

## 5. DECISION GUIDE — ASK IN THIS ORDER FOR EVERY WORD

1. Is it a heading, label, or title? → NO UNDERLINE
2. Is it a standalone named proper noun appearing as its own subject/object? → u-red-zig
3. Is it "and"/"or" sitting between two blue-zig spans? → u-green
4. Is it a short connective preposition (against, between, versus, over) bridging two content noun phrases? → u-green
5. Is it a flow/structural word (linking verb, participial phrase, infinitive, discourse marker, relative clause opener, auxiliary)? → u-green
6. Everything else (nouns, adjectives, facts, outcomes, concepts) → u-blue-zig

### 8C. Keyword generation logic (UPSC MAINS ANSWER-ORIENTED)
The purpose of every generated keyword is NOT easy learning — it is a precise UPSC CSE Mains core keyword for answer-writing. A UPSC aspirant must be able to directly write that bullet title as a Keyword-answers in Mains answer.

Steps:
1. Identify the core examinable point — issue, impact, cause, challenge, measure, outcome.
2. Compress into a precise 2-3 word keyword phrase.
3. Prefer wording drawn from the source explanation.
4. If source wording is too long, compress logically without changing meaning.
5. NEVER use vague one-word labels ("Population", "Awareness", "Inequality") when a sharper point exists.
6. NEVER make keywords a full sentence or long clause.

Examples:
- "reflects increased inequalities in India" → `<strong>Increased Inequalities:</strong>`
- "high levels of malnutrition" → `<strong>Malnutrition Burden:</strong>`
- "decline in their population" → `<strong>Population Decline:</strong>`

**Golden test:** If a UPSC aspirant can write it as-is as a Keyword-answers in Mains answer, it's correct. If it feels vague, too long, too generic, or too explanatory — it's wrong.

### 8D. PYQ alignment is mandatory
Before finalising any keyword, check: has UPSC asked this theme in past papers? If yes, use that exact PYQ phrasing. PYQ vocabulary always beats invented vocabulary.
