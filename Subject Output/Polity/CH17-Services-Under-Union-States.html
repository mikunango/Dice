CH17- Services Under the Union and the States

Part XIV — Article 308 - 323
Article 309
  • Legislative Regulation Power: The appropriate Legislature may regulate the recruitment, and conditions of service of persons appointed, to public services and posts in connection with the affairs of the Union or of any State:
  • Presidential Rule-Making: The President or such person as he may direct in the case of services and posts in connection with the affairs of the Union, and for the Governor of a State or such person as he may direct in the case of services and posts in connection with the affairs of the State, to make rules regulating the recruitment, and the conditions of service of persons appointed, to such services and posts until provision
Article 310
  • Doctrine of Pleasure: (1) Every person who is a member of a defence service or of a civil service of the Union or of an all-India service or holds any post connected with defence or any civil post under the Union holds office during the pleasure of the President.
  • State Civil Service: Every person who is a member of a civil service of a State or holds any civil post under a State holds office during the pleasure of the Governor of the State.
  • Compensation Safeguard: (2) If the President or the Governor, as the case may be, deems it necessary in order to secure the services of a person having special qualifications, provide for the payment to him of compensation, if before the expiration of an agreed period that post is abolished or he is, for reasons not connected with any misconduct on his part, required to vacate that post.
Article 311
  • Removal Safeguard: (1) No person who is a member of a civil service of the Union or an all-India service or a civil service of a State or holds a civil post under the Union or a State shall be dismissed or removed by an authority subordinate to that by which he was appointed.
  • Inquiry Before Removal: (2) No such person as aforesaid shall be dismissed or removed or reduced in rank except after an inquiry in which he has been informed of the charges against him and given a reasonable opportunity of being heard in respect of those charges
    • Penalty Proviso: Provided that where it is proposed after such inquiry, to impose upon him any such penalty, such penalty may be imposed on the basis of the evidence adduced during such inquiry and it shall not be necessary to give such person any opportunity of making representation on the penalty proposed:

Provided further that this clause shall not apply—

  • a. Criminal Conviction: (a) where a person is dismissed or removed or reduced in rank on the ground of conduct which has led to his conviction on a criminal charge; or
  • b. Impracticable Inquiry: (b) where the authority empowered to dismiss or remove a person or to reduce him in rank is satisfied that for some reason, to be recorded by that authority in writing, it is not reasonably practicable to hold such inquiry; or
  • c. Security of State: (c) where the President or the Governor, as the case may be, is satisfied that in the interest of the security of the State it is not expedient to hold such inquiry.

Finality of Decision: (3) If, in respect of any such person as aforesaid, a question arises whether it is reasonably practicable to hold such inquiry as is referred to in clause (2), the decision thereon of the authority empowered to dismiss or remove such person or to reduce him in rank shall be final

ARTICLE 312
  • 1. All-India Services: (1) If the Council of States has declared by resolution supported by not less than two-thirds of the members present and voting that it is necessary or expedient in the national interest so to do, Parliament may by law provide for the creation of one or more all-India services (including an all-India judicial service) common to the Union and the States, and, subject to the other provisions of this Chapter, regulate the recruitment, and the conditions of service of persons appointed, to any such service.
  • 2. Deemed Services: (2) The services known at the commencement of this Constitution as the Indian Administrative Service and the Indian Police Service shall be deemed to be services created by Parliament under this article.
  • 3. Judicial Service Exclusion: (3) The all-India judicial service referred to in clause (1) shall not include any post inferior to that of a district judge as defined in article 236.
  • 4. Amendment Provision: (4) The law providing for the creation of all-India judicial service aforesaid may contain such provisions for the amendment of Chapter VI of Part VI as may be necessary for giving effect to the provisions of that law and no such law shall be deemed to be an amendment of this Constitution for the purposes of article 368.

4. Jurisdiction Bar: (3) Neither the Supreme Court nor any other court shall have jurisdiction in—

  • 1. Covenant Disputes: (a) any dispute arising out of any provision of, or any endorsement on, any covenant, agreement or other similar instrument which was entered into or executed by any person referred to in clause (1), or arising out of any letter issued to such person, in relation to his appointment to any civil service of the Crown in India or his continuance in service under the Government of the Dominion of India or a Province thereof;
  • 2. Article 314 Disputes: (b) any dispute in respect of any right, liability or obligation under article 314 as originally enacted.

5. Overriding Effect: (4) The provisions of this article shall have effect notwithstanding anything in article 314 as originally enacted or in any other provision of this Constitution.

'THE TIME IS RIPE TO REPEAL ARTICLE 311'

1. Protection Provision: Article 311 provides security of tenure and protection against arbitrary dismissal by providing an enquiry process and dismissal by an authority not subordinate to him. However, it acted as a shield against corrupt officials while affecting the morale of honest. Thus, repeal of article 311 needs reconsideration.

Arguments for
  • 1. Anti-Corruption: Reduces corruption and complacency in decision making
  • 2. Administrative Efficiency: Promotes discipline, efficiency and effectiveness of administration
  • 3. Value Promotion: Encourage administrative values like honesty, dedication and motivation
  • 4. Expert Recommendation: ARC report and National Commission for Review of Constitution has also recommended
Arguments against
Page 66
  • 1. Against Natural Justice: Dismissal without inquiry is against the principles of natural justice of right to being heard before accused guilty.
  • 2. Partisan Dismissals: Arbitrary dismissals driven by partisan interests affects neutrality and autonomy of administration
  • 3. Expedite Inquiry: The inquiry process needs expedition than repeal

4. Amendment Needed: Thus, Article 311 should be amended and enquiry proceedings should be expedited in a fair, transparent and time bound manner, to encourage honest officials to act without fear or favour, while punishing the guilty, as envisaged by Sardar Patel.

PUBLIC SERVICE COMMISSIONS

1. Historical Origin: The Public Service Commission's came into existence for the first time in 1926 when the Central Public Service Commission was established under the Government of India Act, 1919. It was renamed as the Federal Public Service Commission after 1st April, 1937, on the introduction of the Government of India Act, 1935.

ARTICLE 315
  • 1. Union & State PSC: (1) Subject to the provisions of this article, there shall be a Public Service Commission for the Union and a Public Service Commission for each State.
  • 2. Joint Commission: (2) Two or more States may agree that there shall be one Public Service Commission for that group of States, and if a resolution to that effect is passed by the House or, where there are two Houses, by each House of the Legislature of each of those States, Parliament may by law provide for the appointment of a Joint State Public Service Commission (referred to in this Chapter as Joint Commission) to serve the needs of those States.
  • 3. Independent Body: The Public Service Commission is an independent statutory body
ARTICLE 316
  • 1. Appointment: (1) The Chairman and other members of a Public Service Commission shall be appointed, in the case of the Union Commission or a Joint Commission, by the President, and in the case of a State Commission, by the Governor of the State:
  • 2. Experience Requirement: It has been further provided that one-half of the members of the Commission, Union or State, must have held office for at least ten years either under the Government of India or the Government of a State.
  • 3. UPSC Strength: ✦ The authorized strength of UPSC including chairman is 10.
  • 4. Term of Office: A member holds office for six years or until he attains, in case of the UPSC, the age of 65 years and in the case of State Commission, the age of 60 years, whichever is earlier.
  • 5. Resignation: A member of a Public Service Commission may, by writing under his hand addressed, in the case of the Union Commission or a Joint Commission, to the President, and in the case of a State Commission, to the Governor of the State, resign his office.
  • 6. Ineligibility: A person who holds office as a member of a Public Service Commission shall, on the expiration of his term of office, be ineligible for re-appointment to that office. (ART. 319)

ARTICLE 317

1. Removal Procedure: (1) Subject to the provisions of clause (3), the Chairman or any other member of a Public Service Commission shall only be removed from his office by order of the President on the ground of misbehaviour after the Supreme Court, on reference being made to it by the President, has, on inquiry held in accordance with the procedure prescribed in that behalf under article 145, reported that the Chairman or such other member, as the case may be, ought on any such ground to be removed.

ARTICLE 318

1. Regulation Power: In the case of the Union Commission or a Joint Commission, the President and, in the case of a State Commission, the Governor of the State may by regulations—

  • (a) Members & Conditions: determine the number of members of the Commission and their conditions of service; and
  • (b) Staff Provisions: make provision with respect to the number of members of the staff of the Commission and their conditions of service:
ARTICLE 321

1. Additional Functions: An Act made by Parliament or, as the case may be, the Legislature of a State may provide for the exercise of additional functions by the Union Public Service Commission or the State Public Service Commission.

ARTICLE 322

1. Charged Expenses: The expenses of the Union or a State Public Service Commission, including any salaries, allowances and pensions payable to or in respect of the members or staff of the Commission, shall be charged on the Consolidated Fund of India or, as the case may be, the Consolidated Fund of the State.

ARTICLE 323
  • 1. Union Commission Report: (1) It shall be the duty of the Union Commission to present annually report to the President.
  • 2. State Commission Report: (2) It shall be the duty of a State Commission to present annually report to the Governor of the State.
