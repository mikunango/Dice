Constitutional Framework and Constituent Assembly

CABINET MISSION PLAN 1946
  • 1. Mission Plan: The Cabinet Mission Plan (May 16, 1946) contained proposals regarding the constitutional future of India.
  • 2. Members: Lord Penthick Lawrence(Secretary of State for India), Sir Stafford Cripps(President of the Board of Trade) and A.V Alexander(First Lord of Admiralty).
  • 3. Mission Arrival: The Mission arrived on 24th March, 1946.
  • 4. Congress Representatives: The congress was represented by Jawaharlal Lal Nehru, Valabhai Patel, Abdul Kalam Azad and Ghaffar Khan.
  • 5. Muslim League Representatives: Muslim League was represented by M.A. Jinnah, Liaquat Ali Khan, Nawab Ismail and Sardar Abdul Rab Nishtar.
OBJECTIVE
  • 1. Constitution-making Body: To formulate a Constitution-making body {Constituent Assembly}.
  • 2. Interim Government: To form the Interim government.
FEATURES
  • 1. Union of India: There should be Union of India—
    • a. Composition: British India and Native States
    • b. Union Subjects: Deal with the subjects of Communication, Defence and Foreign Affairs
    • c. Financial Powers: Have power to raise finance required for the subjects.
  • 2. Provincial Groups: There should be three groups of provinces.
    • a. Group A: Group A, consisting of six Hindu majority provinces; Bombay, Madras, United provinces, Central provinces and Bihar.
    • b. Group B: Group B, consisting of Punjab, Sindh, NWFP and Baluchistan.
    • c. Group C: Bengal and Assam.

  • 3. Union Executive and Legislature: The Union should have an Executive and a Legislature constituted from British Indian and States' representatives.
  • 4. Provincial Subjects: All subjects other than the Union and Residuary should vested in the Provinces.
  • 5. Proportional Representation: To allot to each province a total number of seats proportional to its population, roughly in the ratio of one to a million.
  • 6. Adult Suffrage: Representation by adult suffrage.
INTERIM GOVERNMENT
  • 1. Formation: The interim government of India was formed on September 2, 1946 from the newly elected Constituent Assembly.
  • 2. British Representation: The members of the interim government also include the Viceroy and Commander-in-Chief of Britain

Constituent Assembly
BACKGROUND
  • 1. Gandhi's Vision: Mahatma Gandhi had indicated in 1922 that constitution of India would be drafted by the Indians.
  • 2. Besant's Initiative: Annie Besant called the meeting of the members of central legislative council to discuss about framing the constitution of India.
  • 3. National Demand Resolution: National Demand resolution was introduced by Motilal Nehru on 8 February 1924.
  • 4. Constituent Assembly Idea: M N Roy proposed the idea of a constituent assembly in 1934.
  • 5. Congress Official Demand: The Congress Party put the official demand of constituent assembly in 1936 in Lucknow session.
  • 6. British Acceptance: The British accepted the demand of constituent assembly in the August Offer.
COMPOSITION
  • 1. Indirect Election: Chosen by indirect election by the members of the Provincial Legislative Assemblies.
  • 2. Provincial Members: 292 members were elected through the Provincial Legislative Assemblies.
  • 3. Princely States: 93 members represented the Indian Princely States.
  • 4. Chief Commissioners' Provinces: 4 members represented the Chief Commissioners' Provinces.
  • 5. Initial Strength: Initially, the number of members was 389.
  • 6. Post-Partition Strength: After partition, the number came down to 299.
    • a. British Provinces: 229 were from the British provinces
    • b. Princely States: 70 were nominated from the princely states.

  • 2. President Elected: On December 11, 1946, Dr. Rajendra Prasad was elected as the President.

CRITICISM
  • 1. Not Representative: Not a representative body.
  • 2. No Adult Franchise: Members were not elected by adult franchise.
  • 3. Not Sovereign: Not a sovereign body.
  • 4. Congress Dominance: Dominated by the Congress Party.
  • 5. Complicated Language: Language was complicated.
  • 6. Gandhi's View on Assembly: According to Gandhi ji, Constituent Assembly is the real mirror of the Indian brain.
  • 7. Communal Harmony: According to Gandhi ji, Constituent Assembly would remove the communal unrest.

1. Reflection of Indian Polity: Constituent Assembly is the real mirror of the Indian brain.

2. Communal Harmony as Core Value: Communal harmony and mutual brotherhood is the basic concept of Indian culture. By reaching the last phase of the freedom movement, this concept started taking its original form. The demand of Muslim League for seprate nation and Hindu Muslim riots distracted Gandhiji. Gandhiji accepted Cabinet Mission with pleasure and said that Constituent Assembly would protect the basic concepts of Indian culture and would be organized in such a way that it would reflect the whole of India.

3. Indigenous Constitution: For Gandhi, a Constituent Assembly would not just produce a Constitution indigenous to the country and truly and fully representing the will of the people, but would also solve the communal problem.

4. Mini-India Representation: All communities would be represented in the Assembly in their exact proportion. Therefore the Constituent Assembly was also known as Mini-India because it represented all the major religions and sections of Indian society.

5. Minority Safeguards: The Constituent Assembly recognised that unless safeguards were created, minorities would never be able to gain parity with the majority and basic features of the Constitution and its commitment to a plural and democratic polity would remain mere lip-service.

6. Freedom Movement Direction: Gandhi seemed convinced that the setting up of a Constituent Assembly was the only way out and urged Indian leaders to work towards this goal and steer the freedom movement in this direction.
