CH4- The Preamble

The Preamble
WE, THE PEOPLE OF INDIA, having solemnly resolved to constitute India into a SOVEREIGN SOCIALIST SECULAR DEMOCRATIC REPUBLIC and to secure to all its citizens:
JUSTICE, social, economic and political;
LIBERTY of thought, expression, belief, faith and worship;
EQUALITY of status and of opportunity;
and to promote among them all
FRATERNITY assuring the dignity of the individual and the unity and integrity of the Nation;
IN OUR CONSTITUENT ASSEMBLY this twenty-sixth day of November, 1949, do HEREBY ADOPT, ENACT AND GIVE TO OURSELVES THIS CONSTITUTION

History
  • 1. Objective Resolution: The Preamble to the Indian constitution is based on "Objective Resolution" of Nehru. Jawaharlal Nehru introduced an objective resolution on December 13, 1946, and it was adopted by Constituent assembly on 22 January 1947.
  • 2. Essential Features: The drafting committee felt that the Preamble should be restricted to defining the essential features of the new state and its basic socio-political objectives.
  • 3. Sovereign Democratic Republic: The committee adopted the expression Sovereign Democratic Republic in place of Sovereign Independent Republic as used in the Objective Resolution,.
  • 4. Fraternity Added: The committee added the word Fraternity which was not present in the Objective Resolution.
  • 5. Spirit Embodied: The committee tried to embody in the Preamble the spirit and as far as possible, the language of Objective Resolution.
  • 6. Preamble Drafted: The Preamble was drafted by B. N. Rau in his memorandum of May 30, 1947.
  • 7. Preamble Design: The preamble page, along with other pages of the original Constitution of India, was designed and decorated solely by renowned painter Beohar Rammanohar Sinha of Jabalpur.
Part of the Constitution
BERUBERI CASE

The Preamble is a key to open the mind of the makers but nevertheless the Preamble is not a part of the Constitution.

KESAVANANDA BHARATI CASE

The Preamble is a part of the Constitution and, therefore, is amendable under Article 368.

UNION GOVERNMENT V. LIC OF INDIA

The Preamble is an integral part of the Constitution.

Amendment
  • 1. 42nd Amendment: After Kesavananda Bharati v. State of Kerala, 1973 verdict. Socialist, Secular, and Integrity were added to the preamble by the 42nd Amendment Act, 1976.
Aims & Aspirations

1. Preamble Declaration: A Preamble is a declaration made by the legislature of the reasons for the passage of the statute and is helpful in the interpretation of any ambiguities within the statute to which it is prefixed.

  • 1. Legislative Objectives: Chief Justice Subba Rao in Golak Nath v. State of Punjab had held that "The preamble to an Act sets out the main objectives which the legislation is intended to achieve".
  • 2. Key of Constitution: According to K.M.Munshi, Preamble is the key of the Constitution.
  • 3. Key Words: According to A.Barker, Preamble is the key words of his book.
Preamble is the soul of the Constitution, Justice Hidayatulla

2. Soul of Constitution: The silent features of the Constitution have advanced directly and by implication from these objectives which flow from the Preamble. Preamble as such is broadly acknowledged as the core or soul and spirit of

the constitution. Supreme Court held in Minerva Mills case that it is special because the edifice of our Constitution is built upon the concepts crystallised in the Preamble.

1. Constitutional Morality: It is not a mere solemn resolution, it is something more than a resolution. It is declaration, it is a firm resolve, it is a pledge and an understanding. It is not a spirit of narrow legal wording but a majestic expression of constitutional morality, constitutional polity and constitutional culture.

  • 1. Constitutional Provisions: It records the points and desires of the individuals of India which have been converted into the different provisions of the constitution. It declares India to be a Sovereign Socialist Secular Democratic Republic committed to Justice, Equality and Liberty for the people.
  • 2. Guiding Light: The Preamble does not grant substantive rights and is not enforceable in the courts, but various Indian courts have engaged with the Preamble and have treated it as guiding light in the interpretation of the Constitution.
  • 3. Popular Sovereignty: The opening and last sentences of the Preamble: "We, the people…adopt enact and give to ourselves this Constitution" signifies the power that is vested in the hands of the people.

2. Basic Feature Doctrine: Chief justice Subbarao stated that the Preamble to an act sets out the primary objective with the enactment is proposed to accomplish. Supreme Court held in Smt. Indira Gandhi case that Preamble is the source of any basic feature of the Constitution of India thus ensuring that it is revered, honoured and admired widely by people and Governments at large.

Key Words
We, The People of India
  • 1. People's Constitution: Declares in unambiguous terms that the Constitution has been adopted, enacted and given to themselves by the people of India.
  • 2. Sovereignty Emphasis: It emphasizes the sovereignty of the people.
  • 3. Authors of Constitution: It is the people of India who are the authors of the constitution.
  • 4. Nehru's Statement: Jawahar Lal Nehru in the constituent assembly stated that the word 'People' indicated that the constitution was not created by the States, nor by the people of the several States but by the people of India.
  • 5. Citizens of India: We, the people if India means in other words, we, the citizens of India, whether voters or non-voters.
  • 6. Popular Sovereignty: It also emphasizes the concept of popular sovereignty as laid down by Rousseau.

Sovereign
  • 1. Supreme Power: Sovereignty denotes supreme and ultimate power.
  • 2. Independent Authority: Sovereignty means the independent authority of a state.
  • 3. Spirit of Independence: Sovereign is a reflection of the spirit of independence attained after centuries of subordination to foreign rule.
  • 4. Republican Sovereignty: In the republican form of governments sovereignty is vested to the elected representatives of the people.
  • 5. Irish Origin: According to D.D Basu, the word 'sovereign' is taken from Article 5 of the constitution of Ireland.
  • 6. External Sovereignty: The external sovereignty of India means that it can acquire foreign territory and also cede any part of the Indian territory, subject to limitations(if any) imposed by the constitution.
  • 7. Internal Sovereignty: Internal sovereignty relates to internal and domestic affairs and is divided into four organs, namely, the executive, the legislature, the judiciary and the administrative.

Secular
  • 1. Secular Preamble: The word secular was dropped from the preamble but the Indian constitution has always been secular.
  • 2. 42nd Amendment: Added by the Constitution (42nd Amendment) Act, 1976.
  • 3. Western Origin: The word secular traces its origin from West in the context of the Christian religion.
  • 4. Indian Context: In Indian context, the state does not recognize any religion as a state religion and that it treats all religions equally.
  • 5. S.R.Bommai Case: The Supreme court held in S.R.Bommai case that secularism forms the basic structure of the Indian Constitution.
  • 6. Aruna Roy Case: The Supreme court held in Aruna Roy v. Union of India that secularism has a positive meaning that is developing, understanding and respect towards different religions.
  • 7. I.R Coelho Case: The Supreme court held in I.R Coelho v. State of Tamil Nadu that secularism is a matter of conclusion to be drawn from various Articles conferring Fundamental Rights.
  • 8. Not Irreligion: When India is said to be a secular State, it does not mean that that we exalt irreligion.
  • 9. Article 27 Nature: Article 27 describes the nature of Indian secularism.
    • a. Article 27 Provision: Article 27 provides that no person shall be compelled to pay any taxes, the proceeds whereof are specifically appropriated in payment of expenses for the promotion or maintenance of any particular religion or religious denomination.
  • 10. Religious Freedom: The Supreme Court held the view that a religious institution has freedom to manage its own affairs in matters of religion.
  • 11. DPSP Secularism: In the DPSP, the Articles of 38, 39, 39A, 41 & 46 based on the doctrine of Parens Patriae, promote secularism in all of its form.
  • 12. Egalitarian Cornerstone: It was the cornerstone of an egalitarian and forward-looking society which our constitution endeavored to establish.
Democratic
  • 1. Democracy Etymology: The term Democracy is derived from the Greek words DEMOS which means people and KRATOS which means authority.
  • 2. Lincoln's Definition: Lincoln defined democracy as: Government of the people, by the people, for the people.

  • 3. Mohan Lal Case: The Supreme court held in Mohan Lal v.     • b. Indirect Democracy: In an indirect democracy, the people elect their representatives who carry on the administration of the government directly like India.
Republic
  • 1. Montesquieu Definition: According to Montesquieu, a republican government is that in which a body, or only a part of people, is possessed of the supreme power.
  • 2. Republic Meaning: A republic means a form of government in which the head of the state is an elected(direct or indirect) person and not a heredity monarch.
  • 3. People's Constitution: The Constitution of India is adopted and given to themselves by the People.
  • 4. Ultimate Power: In India, the ultimate power resides in the body of the people exercised via universal adult suffrage.
  • 5. President Election: The president of India who is the executive head of the state is elected by the people (indirectly).
Justice

Social, Economic and Political

  • 1. Welfare State Goal: Justice is considered to be the primary goal of a welfare state and its very existence rests on the parameters of justice.
  • 2. Social Justice: Social justice means the abolition of all sorts of inequities.
  • 3. DPSP Social Justice: To achieve the ideal of social justice, the directives principle for the state policy lays down in Part IV of the constitution.
  • 4. Air India Case: The Supreme court held in Air India Statutory Corporation v. United labour Union case that the aim of social justice was to attain a substantial degree of social, economic and political equality which was the legitimate expectation and constitutional goal.
  • 5. Economic Justice: The economic justice means equal pay for equal work, that every person should get his just dues for his labor irrespective of his caste, sex or social status.
  • 6. Political Justice: Political justice means the absence of any unreasonable or arbitrary distinction among men in political matters.
Liberty

Liberty Of Thought, Expression, Belief, Faith and Worship

  • 1. Liberty Importance: Liberty is Essential to the development of the individuals and the nation.
  • 2. Liberty Definition: Liberty is the power of doing what is allowed by law.
  • 3. Not Mere Absence: Liberty does not mean mere absence of restraint of domination.
  • 4. Not Absolute: It is not abstract or absolute freedom.

  • 5. Regulated Freedom: It is regulated freedom.
  • 6. Controlled by Law: Liberty is confined and controlled by law, whether common law or statute.
  • 7. Part III Liberty: Part III of the constitution(Article - 19,21,25 to28) is related to Liberty.
  • 8. Kartar Singh Case: The Supreme court held in Kartar Singh case that in order, to sustain democracy, liberty is not to degenerate into license.
Equality
  • 1. Equal Enjoyment: Rights carry no meaning if they cannot be enjoyed equally by all members of the community.
  • 2. Abolishing Distinctions: Equality of status and opportunity is secured to the people of India by abolishing all distinctions and discriminations by the state between citizen.
  • 3. Related Articles: Article 15,16, 17,18 are related to equality.
  • 4. Equality of Law: The principle of equality of law means not the same law should apply to everyone, but that a law should deal alike with all in one class.
  • 5. Equal Treatment: Equality of treatment under equal circumstances.
Fraternity
  • 1. Spirit of Brotherhood: Fraternity means the spirit of brotherhood, a feeling that all people are children of the same soil, the same motherland.
  • 2. Democratic Functioning: A democratic system will function in a healthy manner only if there is a spirit of brotherhood, oneness among the people of the land.
  • 3. Individual Dignity: The fraternity is not possible unless the dignity of each individual is preserved and mutually respected.
  • 4. SC on Fraternity: The Supreme Court held that fraternity means a sense of common brotherhood of all Indians.
Dignity
  • 1. Dignity for Fraternity: Dignity of the individuals is to be maintained for the promotion of fraternity.
  • 2. Preamble Assurance: The preamble assures the dignity of each and every individual by securing to each individual equal fundamental rights and by laying down a number of directives for the state.
  • 3. Fundamental Right: The Supreme Court has read the preamble with article 21 to come to the conclusion that the right to dignity is a fundamental right.
