Articles 1-4 | Reorganisation of States

THE UNION & IT'S TEROTORY
  • 1. Article 1 - Union of States: (1) India, that is Bharat, shall be a Union of States.
    • a. First Schedule: (2) The States and the territories thereof shall be as specified in the First Schedule.
    • b. Territory of India: (3) The territory of India shall comprise —
      • i. State Territories: (a) the territories of the States;
      • ii. Union Territories: (b) the Union territories specified in the First Schedule; and
      • iii. Acquired Territories: (c) such other territories as may be acquired.
  • 2. Article 2 - New States: Parliament may by law admit into the Union, or establish, new States on such terms and conditions as it thinks fit.
  • 3. Article 2A - Sikkim: Sikkim to be associated with the Union. Rep. by the Constitution (Thirty-sixth Amendment) Act, 1975, s. 5 (w.e.f. 26-4-1975).
  • 4. Article 3 - Formation of States: Parliament may by law —
    • a. New State Formation: (a) form a new State by separation of territory from any State or by uniting two or more States or parts of States or by uniting any territory to a part of any State;
    • b. Area Increase: (b) increase the area of any State;
    • c. Area Diminish: (c) diminish the area of any State;
    • d. Boundary Alteration: (d) alter the boundaries of any State;
    • e. Name Alteration: (e) alter the name of any State:
  • 5. Proviso - Presidential Recommendation: Provided that no Bill for the purpose shall be introduced in either House of Parliament except on the recommendation of the President and unless, where the proposal contained in the Bill affects the area, boundaries or name of any of the States, the Bill has been referred by the President to the Legislature of that State for expressing its views thereon within such period as may be specified in the reference or within such further period as the President may allow and the period so specified or allowed has expired.
  • 6. Article 4 - Amendment: (1) Any law referred to in article 2 or article 3 shall contain such provisions for the amendment of the First Schedule and the Fourth Schedule as may be necessary to give effect to the provisions of the law and

  • 8. Non-Amendment Clause: (2) No such law as aforesaid shall be deemed to be an amendment of this Constitution for the purposes of article 368.

1. 100th Amendment: The 100th Amendment of the Indian Constitution passed in 2015, established the exchange of some territories between Bangladesh and India. It approved the 1974 Land Boundary Agreement (and its 2011 protocol), which resulted in the exchange of enclaves and the retention of property.

2. 2015 LBA Provisions: Thereby, the 2015 LBA addresses the unsettled issues stemming from the approximately 6.1-kilometre-long land boundary in three sectors: Daikhata-56 (West Bengal), Muhuri River–Belonia (Tripura), and Lathitila–Dumabari (Assam); exchange of enclaves; and adverse possessions, which were first acknowledged in the 2011 Protocol.

DHAR COMMISSION
  • 1. Setup Year: Setup in 1948.
  • 2. Commission Head: S K Dhar was a judge of the Allahabad High Court.
  • 3. Commission Members: The members were Jagat Narain, Panna Lai and B.C. Bannerji,
  • 4. Linguistic Rejection: Rejected the demand of political organization of the country along linguistic lines that it would do immense harm to national unity.
  • 5. Key Considerations: The commission gave more importance to historical, geographical and economic considerations.
  • 6. Reorganisation Basis: The commission favoured reorganization on the basis of –
    • a. Geographical Contiguity: Geographical contiguity and absence of pockets and corridors.
    • b. Financial Self-sufficiency: Financial self-sufficiency.
    • c. Administrative Convenience: Administrative convenience.
    • d. Future Development: Capacity for future development.

MERITS
  • 1. Vernacular Flourishing: The vernacular languages could flourish.
  • 2. Local Participation: Ensure larger participation of the local people.
  • 3. Volatile Situation: Decision to form linguistic states helped to tackle volatile situation.
DEMERITS
  • 1. Regionalism Feeling: Encouraged a feeling of regionalism.
  • 2. Economic Cooperation: Hindered economic cooperation
