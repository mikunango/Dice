HISTORY
  • 1. Vedic Origins: The of Panchayati Raj institution existed since the Vedic period. earliest times.
  • 2. Ancient Texts: Manusmriti, Arthasastra and the Mahabharata mention about it.
  • 3. Kautilya's Account: Arthashastra of Kautilya gives a comprehensive account of the system of village administration.
  • 4. Uttarmerur Inscription: Uttarmerur inscription of Parantaka I gives a comprehensive account of the system of village administration.
  • 5. Mayo's Resolution: Viceroy Lord Mayo's resolution (1870) gave the needed impetus to the development of local institutions.
  • 6. Rippon Resolution: The Rippon Resolution (May 18, 1882) issued by Lord Rippon which focused on towns, provided for local bodies consisting of a large majority of elected non-official members and it was headed by a chairperson, who was also a non-official member.
  • 7. Decentralisation Commission: The Royal Commission on Decentralisation (1907) under the chairmanship of C.E.H. Hobhouse recognised the importance of panchayats at the village level.
  • 8. Montague Chelmsford: The Montague Chelmsford reforms brought a significant development of village Panchayats.
  • 9. Constitutional Provision: After the Constitution came into force, Article 40 made a mention of panchayats and Article 246 empowers the state legislature to legislate with respect to any subject relating to local self-government.

SALIENT FEATURES
  • 1. Constitutional Parts: Added Part IX titled "The Panchayats" (73rd Amendment) and Part IXA titled "The Municipalities" (74th Amendment).
  • 2. Basic Units:
Panchayat Raj Hierarchy Pyramid: Zilla Parishad (District Level) → Anchalik Panchayat (Block Level) → Gaon Panchayats (Village Level) → Gram Sabha / Ward Sabha (Panchayat / Municipal Level)
  • 3. Direct Elections: Seats at all levels to be filled by direct elections Article 243C (2).
  • 4. SC/ST Reservation: Seats reserved for Scheduled Castes (SCs) and Scheduled Tribes (STs) and the chairpersons of the Panchayats at all levels also shall be reserved for SCs and STs in proportion to their population. (Article 243D)
  • 5. Women Reservation: One-third of the total number of seats to be reserved for women. (Article 243D)
  • 6. SC/ST Women Seats: One third of the seats reserved for SCs and STs also reserved for women.
  • 7. Women Chairpersons: One-third offices of chairpersons at all levels reserved for women (Article 243D).
  • 8. Five Year Term: Uniform five year term and elections to constitute new bodies to be completed before the expiry of the term.
  • 9. Dissolution Elections: In the event of dissolution, elections compulsorily within six months (Article 243E).
  • 10. Election Commission: Independent Election Commission in each State for superintendence, direction and control of the electoral rolls (Article 243K).
  • 11. Economic Development Plans: Panchayats to prepare plans for economic development and social justice in respect of subjects as devolved by law to the various levels of Panchayats including the subjects as illustrated in Eleventh Schedule (Article 243G).
  • 12. District Planning: 74th Amendment provides for a District Planning Committee to consolidate the plans prepared by Panchayats and Municipalities (Article 243ZD).
  • 13. Revenue Sources: Budgetary allocation from State Governments, share of revenue of certain taxes, collection and retention of the revenue it raises, Central Government programmes and grants, Union Finance Commission grants (Article 243H).
  • 14. State Finance Commission: Establish a Finance Commission in each State to determine the principles on the basis of which adequate financial resources would be ensured for panchayats and municipalities (Article 243I).
  • 15. Eleventh Schedule: The Eleventh Schedule of the Constitution places as many as 29 functions within the purview of the Panchayati Raj bodies.

Article 243F: Article 243F makes provisions for disqualifications from the membership.

  • 1. MLA Qualification Parity: As per this article, any person who is qualified to become an MLA is qualified to become a member of the Panchayat.
  • 2. Minimum Age: For Panchayat the minimum age prescribed is 21 years.
  • 3. State Legislature Role: The disqualification criteria are to be decided by the state legislature by law.

Panchayat Samiti Income: The main source of income of the panchayat samiti is grants-in-aid and loans from the respective State Governments.

ISSUES
  • 1. Inadequate Funds: Lack of adequate funds.
  • 2. Structural Deficiencies: Suffer from structural deficiencies.
  • 3. Manpower Shortage: Lack of manpower in the PRIs.
  • 4. SFC Recommendations: Recommendations of State Finance Commissions (SFCs) are generally not taken seriously.
  • 5. Women Representation Issue: While women got political representation, the real power was usurped by their husbands.
  • 6. Non-Mandated Transfer: The transfer of various governance functions—like the provision of education, health, sanitation, and water was not mandated.
  • 7. Taxation Power: The power to tax, even for subjects falling within the purview of PRIs, has to be specifically authorized by the state legislature.
  • 8. MP/MLA Interference: Interference of area MPs and MLAs in the functioning of panchayats.
  • 9. Illiteracy & Ignorance: Widespread illiteracy and ignorance.
  • 10. Gram Sabha Agenda: Lack of clear setting of agenda in gram sabha, gram samiti meetings and no proper structure.
  • 11. Planning Limitations: No secretarial support and lower levels of technical knowledge which restricted the aggregation of bottom up planning.
CONSTITUTIONALISATION OF PANCHAYATI RAJ PAVED THE WAY FOR GRASS ROOT EMPOWERMENT IN INDIA. IN THIS REGARD DISCUSS THE SALIENT FEATURES OF THE 73RD AMENDMENT ACT, 1992.
"Decentralisation both in the political and economic sphere is absolutely essential if India is to function as the democracy". Gandhi Ji

73rd Amendment Overview: The 73rd amendment Act, 1992 has given practical shape to the Directive Principles of State Policy by constitutionalizing the local governance through establishment of Panchayati Raj in India, which transformed the representative democracy into a participatory one. Its salient features are:

Page 46
  • 1. Three-Tier Structure: In every state, the panchayat will be at three levels i.e, village, intermediate and district level.
  • 2. Village Assembly: Village assembly comprises of all registered voters in the area of a panchayat and serves as the foundation of Panchayati Raj. The powers and functions of a Gram Sabha are determined by the respective state legislatures.
  • 3. Direct Election: All members at all levels are directly elected by people. The chairperson of panchayat at village level is elected as per provisions of the state legislature and chairperson of panchayat at remaining two tiers are elected indirectly by and from amongst elected members.
  • 4. SC/ST Reservation: There is proportionate reservation for SCs and STs at every level of the Panchayati Raj. Also, women are provided with minimum one-third reservations at all the levels of panchayats. The state legislature may also make provisions for reservations of OBCs.
  • 5. Five-Year Term: A panchayat is given a five-year term, but it can be dissolved before its completion of the term. A definite timeline is given for holding fresh elections and if a panchayat is dissolved prematurely, then the reconstituted panchayat will hold office only for the remaining period.
  • 6. State Election Commission: The conduct of Panchayati Raj elections is vested in the state election commission consisting of a state election commissioner appointed by the Governor of the state.
  • 7. Finance Commission: The Governor of a state will, after every five years, constitute a finance commission to review the financial position of panchayats.

Performance Challenges: However, the performance of Panchayats has lot left to be desired, especially in light of limited powers and financial autonomy provided to them by the States. A lot can be done to improve the performance of panchayats, including greater financial autonomy, capacity building, infrastructure improvement and less bureaucratic control. To make it truly participatory, the 3Fs (Functions, Funds, And Functionaries) of Panchayati Raj needs to be strengthened.
